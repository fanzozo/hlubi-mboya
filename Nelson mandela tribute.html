NELSON MANDELA

He was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary, political leader, and philanthropist who spent 27 years as political prisoner before becoming, and a remarkable twist the country's first black president.

Here's a time line of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born in Mveso, Cape province, South Africa
  • 1938 - Attends University of Fort Hare
  • 1944 - First Marriage
  • 1944 - Forms the A.N.C Youth League
  • 1952 - Mandela starts law practise
  • 1956 - Mandela arrested on treason charges, by apartheid goverment
  • 1958 - Second marriage, during trial
  • 1961 - Acquitted of treason
  • 1961 - Mandela helps form querrilla army
  • 1962 - Mandela arrested again
  • 1964 - Sentenced to life in prison
  • 1990 - Mandela freed
  • 1992 - Breakup of second marriage
  • 1993 - Mandela awarded with the Nobel Peace Price
  • 1994 - Mandela becomes President
  • 1998 - Third marriage at 80
  • 1999 - Succeeded by Thabo Mbeki
  • 2013 - Dies at the age 95.
